VIRTUAL TRAVELLING

Travelling is said to be one of the best and most effective remedies in case someone is having a hard time or life is just not going their way. It is a great stress reliever and it refreshes or boosts ones metabolism. Everyone needs a change of pace, a different scenery sometimes in their life. Sometimes life does not work out the way one wants it to. And it is crucial for that person to take some time out and either travel to another place or just go out on a long drive.

So what if you can't travel the world due to specific reasons, Travel your dream place virtually.

MUSEUMS

These museums are offering free virtual tours

1. The Louvre

Explore Egyptian antiquities and travel to another era as you encounter the remains of the Louvre’s Moat in these comprehensive virtual tours.
2. Van Gogh Museum

The tour, a part of Google’s Art and Culture Network, takes you through the Van Gogh Museum allowing you to view the artworks up close.
3. The Vatican Museum

The Vatican Museum website has seven virtual tours including a 360-degree view of the famous Sistine Chapel.
4. Metropolitan Museum of Art

Stroll virtually around the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York and click on each exhibit to find out more about them.
5. British Museum

Travel through time via your mouse or arrow keys via an interactive game brought to you by the British Museum. Informative audio descriptions help virtually tour the museum’s exhibits
6. Rijksmuseum

This famous museum in Amsterdam houses legendary works by Rembrandt, Van Gogh, and other Dutch masters. Click on the virtual tour for all the must-see exhibits
7. Musée d’Orsay

See works from Van Gogh, Monet and Cezanne, as you yake a look around this railway station-covered-museum. Try to guess which works belong to which artists.
8. Alimentarium

The Museum is devoted to food and nutrition and takes visitors on an interactive journey through the world of food, challenging the brain and testing all of the senses. The journey continues online, where pioneering teaching and learning assets are available for those who want to further explore the world of food.
